## Why this exists (not shown to users — reasoning for you)

This isn't boilerplate CYA — it does three real things: (1) it's the
trademark-safe way to describe compatibility without implying official
partnership, (2) it puts the ban-risk decision explicitly in the user's
hands with an affirmative checkbox (much stronger than a buried EULA clause
if this is ever disputed), and (3) it's honest marketing — the "we don't
touch game memory" claim is something we can actually stand behind per the
architecture review, not a hollow promise.
